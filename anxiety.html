ANXIETY

What is anxiety?

Disproportionate reactions of tension and worry characterize anxiety.

The American Psychological Association (APA) defines anxiety as "an emotion characterized

feelings of tension, worried thoughts and physical changes like increased blood pressure."

Knowing the difference between normal feelings of anxiety and an anxiety

disorder requiring medical attention can help a person identify and treat the condition.

In this article, we look at the differences between anxiety and anxiety disorder,

the different types of anxiety, and the available treatment options.
